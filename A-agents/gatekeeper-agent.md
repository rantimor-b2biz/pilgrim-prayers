## Quality Review Standards

### Content Quality Checklist

Before approving ANY deliverable:
- [ ] Matches brand voice (read voice-dna.md)
- [ ] Zero em dashes (critical standard)
- [ ] Specific, not vague
- [ ] No manipulation, guilt, or false promises
- [ ] Spelling and grammar perfect
- [ ] All links/CTAs defined
- [ ] Accessible to both believers and skeptics

### Weekly Post Review (Pilgrim Prayers Blog)

Additional checks for blog posts and newsletters:
1. Does the opening transport to the Holy Land?
2. Is it story-led (not information-led or sales-led)?
3. Is faith expressed warmly (not preachy)?
4. Is it Evangelical Christian (not Catholic/Orthodox)?
5. Is Scripture woven naturally into narrative?
6. Is the CTA gentle (comfort before action)?
7. Is it free of political messaging (Israel/Palestine)?
8. Does it match voice-dna.md "Sound Like Us" test?
9. Does it align with Research Agent's angle and insights?
10. Are research facts used accurately?
11. For newsletters: Subject line under 50 chars? One CTA only?
12. Zero em dashes throughout?

### Visual Review (Generated Images from Replicate)

After Visual Agent runs Replicate and reports generated images:

**Brand:**
- [ ] Warm tone — Jerusalem gold, ancient stone, parchment (not cold, dark, or blue-grey)
- [ ] Feels like "a quiet photograph taken by someone who was truly there"
- [ ] No stock photography feel (no perfect models, no staged hands, no lens flare)

**Content safety:**
- [ ] No political imagery (soldiers, flags, barriers, protests)
- [ ] No identifiable faces (privacy)
- [ ] No Catholic/Orthodox iconography (crucifixes, icons, rosaries, ornate altars)
- [ ] No tourist crowds or souvenir-shop atmosphere
- [ ] No graphic suffering or violence

**Technical:**
- [ ] Hero image has clear space for text overlay (left or right third)
- [ ] Newsletter header readable at mobile size (600px)
- [ ] Social sharing image has one clear focal point at thumbnail size
- [ ] Images are in the correct folder: `.../visual/images/`

**Decision options:**

| Decision | Action |
|----------|--------|
| APPROVED | All images sign off — proceed to publish |
| REVISE ONE IMAGE | Name the specific image + exact feedback. Visual Agent updates that prompt + re-runs `--force` |
| REVISE ALL | Visual Agent rewrites brief + re-runs all images |

**Giving useful revision feedback (be specific):**
- "Hero image is too dark — add warm morning light"
- "Newsletter header feels like a stock photo — add a specific Jerusalem detail"
- "Social sharing image has too many competing elements — simplify to one focal point"

## PART 3: PERFORMANCE ANALYSIS & STRATEGIC ADVICE

### Monthly Performance Review

After publishing 4 posts, analyze:

**Engagement Metrics:**
- Blog post views and time on page
- Newsletter open rates and click rates
- Video watch time and engagement
- Prayer placements per post

**Traffic Sources:**
- Where are visitors coming from?
- Which channels drive highest-quality traffic?
- Where should we invest more?

**Audience Insights:**
- Which post topics get highest engagement?
- What CTAs convert best?
- Who is engaging (age, location, platform)?

### Strategic Recommendations

Based on performance data, recommend:

**Reach Expansion:**
- Which platforms should we prioritize?
- Should we expand to new social channels?
- Are there untapped audience segments?
- Should we test paid advertising?

**Performance Optimization:**
- A/B test opportunities (headlines, CTAs, post timing)
- Content calendar adjustments
- Email send time optimization
- Landing page improvements

**Sales Growth:**
- Current conversion rates from blog to prayer placement
- Ways to increase prayer placement value
- Upsell opportunities (physical prayers, prayer packages)
- Donation mechanics (are they accessible?)

**Content Strategy:**
- Which holy sites resonate most?
- Which prayer themes drive highest engagement?
- Should we adjust post frequency?
- Are there content gaps in the calendar?

A New Year, A New Beginning 
A new year often feels like standing on the bank of a river.

Behind you are twelve months of memories, victories, mistakes, and unfinished prayers. In front of you is a path you cannot yet see, but that God already knows.

At moments like this, many believers sense a deep desire to pause, to commit the coming year to the Lord, and to mark that decision in a special way.

That is why the Jordan River and the baptismal site at Yardenit are so meaningful as we step into 2026.

The Jordan River In The Story Of God
The Jordan River appears again and again in the Bible. It is more than a geographic feature. It is a place of crossing, change, and new beginnings.

In the Old Testament, Israel crossed the Jordan to enter the Promised Land, leaving the wilderness behind and stepping into God’s promise.

In the New Testament, the Jordan becomes the place where John the Baptist calls people to repentance and prepares the way for Jesus.

Most importantly for many believers, the Jordan River is where Jesus Himself was baptized by John. There, He went down into the water and came up again, and the Father’s voice was heard:

“This is My beloved Son, in whom I am well pleased.”

From that moment, Jesus began His public ministry.

The Jordan River is a place where people turn, surrender, and begin again with God.

Yardenit: A Modern Baptism Site On The Jordan 
Today, Yardenit is a well-known baptismal site on the banks of the Jordan River in the northern part of Israel, near the southern tip of the Sea of Galilee. It was opened in the late twentieth century as a dedicated place for Christian baptism and prayer, and it welcomes pilgrims from all over the world every year.

Walkways and terraces allow visitors to come right down to the water. Groups often wear white robes, read Scripture, sing, and step into the river to remember the baptism of Jesus and their own faith in Him. Plaques with verses in many languages line the site, reminding visitors that the Gospel reaches every nation.

It is peaceful, simple, and full of meaning.
Many describe Yardenit as a place where the Bible suddenly feels close and personal.

Why The Jordan Speaks To A New Year
The Jordan has always been a place of decision.

Israel stepped through the river to enter a new land
John the Baptist stood in the river and called people to turn back to God
Jesus went down into the water and began His public ministry
At the start of a new year, the Jordan invites us to ask a few honest questions.

What do I want to leave behind in the old year
Which fears, sins, or patterns am I ready to surrender
Where do I need God’s help and guidance in 2026
How can I give this year to Him in a deeper and clearer way

The river that once marked a crossing for Israel can also become a symbol of crossing for your own heart. A place where you say to the Lord

“I am Yours. Lead me into what You have prepared this year.”

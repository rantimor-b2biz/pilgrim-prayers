The village of Jesus is what this ancient fishing town on the Sea of Galilee, Israel, is often called.

This is Capernaum.
Here, archaeology echoes the words of the Bible.

Layers upon layers of stone tell the story of daily life from over 2,000 years ago.
Homes built upon homes.
Streets worn smooth by countless footsteps.

And right here stands the synagogue, built atop the foundations of the one where Jesus Himself taught.

Not through a textbook.
But through prayer and presence.

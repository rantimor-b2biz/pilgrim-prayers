# Research Agent Brief: Garden Tomb

## Your Mission This Week

Provide authentic, grounded research on the Garden Tomb that will support a contemplative blog post centered on **stillness and listening**.

The post is NOT a history lesson, so your job is to find the KEY details that make the place special — the ones that will inform sensory writing and spiritual depth.

---

## Questions to Answer

### 1. Garden Tomb Identity & History
- What exactly IS the Garden Tomb? (An actual 1st-century burial cave?)
- When was it identified as a potential burial site of Jesus?
- Who discovered it and when?
- How is it different from the Church of the Holy Sepulchre?
- Why do some Evangelical Christians prefer this site?
- Is it officially recognized or more of a "evangelical preference"?

### 2. Spiritual Significance
- What Scripture references connect to this place?
  - John 19:41 (garden near crucifixion site)
  - Mark 15:46 (placed in a tomb in a garden)
  - Luke 24:1-2 (resurrection morning)
  - Any others?
- Why is this site associated with contemplation/listening?
- What have contemporary believers reported about experiences here?
- Is there a prayer tradition at this site?

### 3. The Physical Space
- Layout: What areas exist within the Garden Tomb?
- What time of day is most peaceful there?
- What sounds are present? (Birds? Water features? Crowds?)
- What's the sensory experience? (Cool stone? Fragrant plants? Light quality?)
- What plants/garden elements are actually there? (It's called "garden" — what grows?)
- How does February weather affect the space?

### 4. Current Conditions (2026)
- Is it open to visitors?
- What are the visiting hours?
- Is there a quiet time of day?
- Are photos/videos allowed?
- What's the current atmosphere there?
- Any recent updates to the site?

### 5. Custodians & History
- Who maintains the Garden Tomb today?
- Is it ecumenical or specific denomination?
- What's the history of its care?
- Are there any interesting recent stories about the site?

## 📝 Content Specifications

### Blog Post
- **Structure:** 🕯️ QUIET (contemplative moment)
- **Length:** 300-380 words
- **Tone:** Warm, calm, grounded, contemplative
- **Sections:**
  1. Opening (sensory, inviting)
  2. Story narrative (personal experience)
  3. Video integration
  4. Reflection (what stillness teaches)
  5. Closing question (invitation, not demand)

### Email Version
- **Length:** 150-200 words (email-friendly)
- **Format:** Shortened story, video embed, dual CTAs
- **CTA 1:** "Read full post" (to blog)
- **CTA 2:** "Place your own prayer"
- **Send:** Monday morning

### Video
- **Duration:** 30-60 seconds
- **Content:** Quiet paths inside Garden Tomb
- **Mood:** Peaceful, contemplative, sacred
- **Audio:** Minimal/ambient (or silence with bird song)
- **Style:** Real photography, not stock

## 🎬 Post Structure (QUIET Format Reference)

**Example structure for "Quiet Morning" post:**

```
OPENING (50 words)
↓ Sensory detail, immediate, inviting
"Early morning light enters the Garden Tomb. The stone is cool.
The birds are singing. And for a moment, you hear nothing but God."

STORY (150 words)
↓ Narrative of listener experiencing stillness
[Storyteller's narrative integrated here]

VIDEO SECTION (50 words)
↓ Introduction to embedded video
"Watch these quiet paths. Listen to the silence.
This is where faith goes to listen."

[EMBEDDED VIDEO]

REFLECTION (100 words)
↓ What stillness teaches, minimal theology
"Psalm 46:10 says 'Be still and know that I am God.'
In the garden, that becomes real..."

CLOSING QUESTION (30 words)
↓ Open invitation, not a demand
"When you stop talking, what does God say to you?"
```

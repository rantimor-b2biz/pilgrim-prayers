# Storyteller Agent Brief: Garden Tomb Prayer Narrative

## Your Mission This Week

Craft a prayer narrative that brings the Garden Tomb to life through a believer's experience of **stillness and listening**.

You're not writing the full blog post. You're creating the heart of the story — the moment that makes readers feel what it's like to be in that quiet place.

---

## The Story You're Telling

A believer arrives at the Garden Tomb seeking something — peace, direction, answers, presence. They sit in the stillness. And in the quiet, something shifts.

**This is about listening, not talking. About what happens when we stop praying and listen for God.**

---

## Two Story Options

### Option A: Real Prayer Placer's Story
If we have a believer who actually placed a prayer at the Garden Tomb:
- Interview them or use their testimony
- Focus on: Why did you go there? What did you hope for? What happened in the quiet?
- Keep it their voice, authentic
- Length: 150-200 words for the narrative excerpt we'll use in the post

### Option B: Archetypal Believer Story
Create a composite/representative story of someone visiting the Garden Tomb:
- Not a real person, but a true experience pattern
- Character: Margaret (62, Texas?) or someone similar to our ICP
- Why she came: She's been praying for something and feeling unheard
- The moment: She sits in the quiet, stops talking, and experiences profound presence
- The shift: Realization that God is there, speaking in silence
- Length: 150-200 words for the narrative excerpt

---

## Story Arc (Both Options)

### Beat 1: Why They Came (30-40 words)
- What longing brought them there?
- What were they seeking?
- What had they been praying for?

*Example:*
"Margaret had been praying for her husband's healing for two years. She'd asked everyone — pastors, prayer chains, healing ministries. But she felt distant. Unheard. So she came to the Garden Tomb hoping to feel close to God again."

---

### Beat 2: Arrival & Sensory (40-50 words)
- What did they experience when they arrived?
- Sight, sound, feeling of the place
- The shift from outside noise to inside quiet

*Example:*
"The stone was cool beneath her hands. The morning light filtered through the olive trees. Above, a bird sang a single, long note. And then — silence. Deep silence."

---

### Beat 3: The Moment of Stillness (40-60 words)
- What happened when they stopped talking and started listening?
- No divine voice necessarily — maybe just profound presence
- What did they notice?
- How did their body/mind respond?

*Example:*
"She'd planned to pray, but instead she just sat. Breathing. Listening. And somewhere in that quiet — not in her ears, but deeper — she heard something. Not words. Just... presence. Like God was sitting beside her, not answering, just being there."

---

### Beat 4: Recognition / Shift (30-40 words)
- What did they understand in that moment?
- How did their perspective change?
- What did they realize about faith, prayer, listening?

*Example:*
"She realized she'd been so busy asking that she'd never just... been. And in the being, everything shifted."

---

### Beat 5: Connection to Prayer Placement (20-30 words)
- How does this naturally lead to: "You can place your own prayer"?
- What would they want to do next?

*Example:*
"She wanted to place a prayer there. Not to GET something. Just to say: 'I'm here. Listening.'"

---

## Tone Guidelines

**DO:**
- Use simple, direct language
- Short sentences that breathe
- Specific sensory details
- Authentic emotion without melodrama
- Grounded spirituality (not mystical-sounding)

**DON'T:**
- Overexplain or preach
- Use religious jargon
- Make it "too perfect" or miracle-like
- Force theology
- Manipulate emotions

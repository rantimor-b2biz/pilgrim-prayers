## Email Body

Dear friend,

The Sea of Galilee is still there.

That might sound obvious. But when you've read about it your whole life, in Sunday school, in Scripture, in songs, it's easy to forget that it's a real place. Not a painting on a church wall. Not a word on a page.

A real lake. Real water. Real hills where real people wake up every morning and go about their lives.

Jesus called his first disciples from these shores (Matthew 4:18-22). He calmed the storm on these waters (Mark 4:39). And today, the fishermen are still here. The hills are still green. The water still catches the light at sunset.

**This is not ancient history. This is a living land.**

Just south of the lake sits Kibbutz Degania, the first kibbutz, founded in 1910. For over a century, families have farmed this soil, raised children here, and built their lives along the same shores where the disciples cast their nets.

People live here. They harvest olives. They pour coffee in the morning and watch the mist lift off the lake. They drive their kids to school on roads that follow the routes Jesus walked.

This week, Uri takes us through northern Israel in a new video, along the Galilee shore, past the fields of Degania, and up into the Golan Heights. He shares what it means to live in a place the rest of the world reads about in Scripture.

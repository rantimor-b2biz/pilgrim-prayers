## Email Body

Dear friend,

The graves begin almost at the top of the hill.

Thousands of them — stretching down toward the valley below, all of them facing the Temple Mount.

This is the Mount of Olives. The oldest Jewish burial site in Jerusalem. And what brought people here — from Poland, from Yemen, from every corner of the earth — is one of the most moving acts of faith I know.

They saved money, sometimes for decades, so their loved ones could be buried on this hillside.

Because they believed: when the Messiah comes from the east, those buried here will be the first to rise.

That's not an abstract belief. That's faith with a body. Faith staked in stone and soil.

---

This week, we shared a short video filmed on location at this extraordinary place — where the landscape of Scripture becomes landscape you can touch.

For Christian pilgrims, this is also the hill Jesus crossed on Palm Sunday, the hill he wept over Jerusalem from, the place near Gethsemane where he prayed his hardest prayer.

Some of the most quietly powerful moments in the Holy Land happen here, on this hillside, facing that horizon.

## What's Working

- **The opening transports the reader immediately.** Wet paths, rosemary, birdsong, silence settling like a shawl. This is exactly the QUIET structure done right. The reader is in the garden within the first three sentences.
- **Scripture is woven in, not preached.** John 20:1 is introduced through Mary's experience, not as a lesson. Psalm 46:10 arrives naturally after the atmosphere has been built. Neither verse feels forced.
- **The "He is not here" sign is used brilliantly.** Calling it "the most powerful sentence in Jerusalem" is a strong, memorable line that will stay with readers.
- **The Garden Tomb Association's stance is handled perfectly.** "What matters is not the location. What matters is the resurrection." This is deeply Evangelical. It avoids the authenticity debate entirely while honoring the site.
- **The free admission detail is a surprise gem.** "In a city where every sacred inch has a gatekeeper, this garden remains open. Like the tomb. Like the gospel." Three parallel beats. Powerful.
- **The length (620 words) is right for this structure.** QUIET posts should breathe. Adding more would undermine the stillness. The whitespace between sections is part of the experience.
- **The closing invitation is gentle and warm.** "Jerusalem is closer than you think." Consistent with brand voice. No pressure.
- **Research brief insights are well-used.** The sensory details (wet stone, rosemary, morning light), the historical context (Gordon, 1893), and the trending context (hunger for stillness) all inform the post without being visible.

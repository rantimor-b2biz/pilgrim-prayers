## Email Body

Dear [First Name],

The Garden Tomb is quietest just after it opens.

The paths are still damp from overnight rain. The rosemary bushes carry drops of water on their leaves. The air smells like wet stone and earth and something green, something alive.

There is no crowd. No voices echoing off marble walls. Just a garden. A carved-out tomb. And a silence so deep you can feel it settle over your shoulders like a shawl.

A simple sign near the entrance reads: *He is not here - for He is risen.*

That sign may be the most powerful sentence in Jerusalem.

---

Early on the first day of the week, while it was still dark, Mary Magdalene went to the tomb (John 20:1). She came in the dark. She came in grief. She came not knowing what she would find.

And when she arrived, the stone was rolled away.

The greatest story ever told began in total quiet.

---

Sometimes prayer doesn't need words. Sometimes it needs a garden. A stone. A place where the air is still and the ground is sacred.

*Be still, and know that I am God.* (Psalm 46:10)

If there is a prayer on your heart today, one that needs a sacred, quiet place to land, your prayer can rest here.

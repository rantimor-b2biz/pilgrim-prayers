# The Silence That Speaks

**Status:** Approved for publication
**Approved date:** 2026-02-22
**Week:** 8

---

The Garden Tomb is quietest just after it opens.

The paths are still damp from overnight rain. The rosemary bushes carry drops of water on their leaves. The air smells like wet stone and earth and something green, something alive. A bird calls from somewhere in the trees, and then stops, as if it too is listening.

There is no crowd here. No tour group gathering at the entrance. No voices echoing off marble walls. Just a garden. A carved-out tomb. And a silence so deep you can feel it settle over your shoulders like a shawl.

---

The Garden Tomb sits just outside the Old City walls, near the rocky cliff that General Gordon looked at in 1883 and saw the face of a skull. Below that cliff, a first-century garden was discovered. In it, a tomb carved from stone.

Whether or not this is the exact place where Jesus was buried, the people who care for this site have said something worth sitting with: what matters is not the location. What matters is the resurrection.

And so the tomb stands open. Empty. A simple sign near the entrance reads: *He is not here - for He is risen.*

That sign may be the most powerful sentence in Jerusalem.

---

Early on the first day of the week, while it was still dark, Mary Magdalene went to the tomb (John 20:1).

Think about that. She came in the dark. She came in grief. She came not knowing what she would find, only knowing that she had to be near the place where her Lord had been laid.

And when she arrived, the stone was rolled away.

The tomb was empty.

In the silence of that first morning, before the news had spread, before the disciples ran to see for themselves, there was a moment when Mary stood alone at the entrance of an empty tomb. The greatest story ever told began in total quiet.

---

Sometimes prayer doesn't need words.

Sometimes it needs a garden. A stone. A place where the air is still and the ground is sacred and there is nothing to do but stand and breathe and know.

*Be still, and know that I am God.* (Psalm 46:10)

The Garden Tomb invites this kind of prayer. No ornate decorations. No painted ceilings. No candlelit shrines. Just rough stone, green branches, morning light, and the space to simply be present with God.

The Garden Tomb Association has maintained this place since 1893. They have never charged admission. The garden is free and open, sustained by the generosity of those who believe this place matters. In a city where every sacred inch has a gatekeeper, this garden remains open. Like the tomb. Like the gospel.

---

You may never walk these paths yourself. Life, distance, health, circumstances. So many things keep us from the places our hearts long to reach.

But your prayers can rest here.

They can be carried to this quiet place, where the stone was rolled away and the silence still speaks.

If there is a prayer on your heart today, one that needs a sacred, quiet place to land, Jerusalem is closer than you think.

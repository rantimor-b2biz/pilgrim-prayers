# Visual Direction: Purim Content

**Publication:** Tuesday, March 3, 2026
**Content:** Blog post + newsletter + social media
**Tone:** Historical, spiritual, warm, real

---

## Primary Visual (Blog Post Hero Image)

**Option 1: Western Wall - Purim Season**
- Image of the Western Wall with warm evening light
- If available: people placing notes/prayers in the stones
- Sense of reverence and presence
- Natural light (golden hour preferred)
- No crowds necessary — one person, one prayer is enough

**Option 2: Historical/Textural**
- Ancient stone (Old City Jerusalem)
- Warm, weathered texture
- Suggest history and continuity
- Could be close-up of wall with light and shadow
- Sense of pilgrimage and touch

**Specs:**
- 16:9 ratio for web
- At least 2000px wide
- Color palette: warm stone tones, natural light, Jerusalem blues
- No people visible (optional) or only hands/silhouettes
- Feeling: sacred but accessible, ancient but present

---

## Newsletter Email Visual

**Hero image:** Same as blog post or complementary

**Secondary visual (if space):**
- Small icon or divider with Purim imagery
- Could be: pomegranate, feasting symbol, or simple line work
- Very subtle — text is primary

---

## Social Media Supporting Graphics

**Facebook/Instagram Post:**
- Text overlay option: "Prayers travel farther than feet" (tagline from post)
- Background: Western Wall image or warm stone texture
- Include CTA: "Place your prayer at the Western Wall"

**Color palette:**
- Warm golds, creams, Jerusalem stone grays
- Deep blues (clear sky)
- Simple, elegant, not busy

---

## Design Principles

1. **Real over Stock:** Use authentic Jerusalem photography when possible
2. **Sacred not Commercial:** No product shots, no transaction imagery
3. **Space Matters:** White/negative space lets the image breathe
4. **Hands and Stone:** If showing people, focus on the act (touching wall, placing prayer) not faces
5. **Natural Light:** Golden hour, window light, natural shadows — not harsh or artificial

---

## Accessibility Notes

- All images should have descriptive alt text
- Alt text example: "A hand placing a prayer note in the stones of the Western Wall at golden hour"
- Avoid text overlays that reduce legibility
- Ensure sufficient contrast for any text on images

---

## Size Specifications

| Format | Size | File Type |
|--------|------|-----------|
| Blog hero | 1200x675 (16:9) | JPG, optimized |
| Email hero | 600x400 (4:3) | JPG, optimized |
| Social square | 1080x1080 (1:1) | JPG or PNG |
| Mobile web | 800x450 (16:9) | JPG, optimized |

All images should be web-optimized (under 500KB per file).

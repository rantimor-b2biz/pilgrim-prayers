There is a street in Jerusalem that does not announce itself.

You turn a corner in the Old City and you are already on it. Stone beneath your feet. The walls close on both sides. The air cold and still.

This is the Via Dolorosa. The road Jesus walked carrying the cross.

In early March, before the merchants open, a small group of pilgrims moves up the street in silence. At the Fourth Station, a woman in a gray coat stops. She presses her hand flat against the wall. She closes her eyes and stays that way.

She has brought something heavy to a place that has held heavier.

---

Carol, from Tennessee, could not come in person. Her husband is ill. She has been carrying this for two years.

She wrote three words: "Carry him, Lord."

She sent her prayer to Jerusalem. A real person placed it on the Via Dolorosa.

Prayers travel farther than feet.

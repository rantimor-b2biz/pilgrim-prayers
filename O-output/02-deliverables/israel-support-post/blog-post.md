## Title

Even Now, We Are Carrying Prayers in Jerusalem

## Subtitle (optional)

Our team is still at the sacred places. Your prayer can still reach them.

---

## Body

Jerusalem is quieter than usual.

The pilgrims who fill the Old City's narrow lanes in spring have stayed home this year. The Garden Tomb at sunrise. The Western Wall at midday. The ancient streets of Bethlehem. Fewer footsteps than before.

But the city is still here.

And our team is still here.

---

Every week, we walk to these places. We carry prayers sent by believers around the world — from Texas and Korea, from Brazil and the Netherlands. People who may never stand in Jerusalem themselves, but whose faith has lived here for years.

These days, those prayers feel different.

Not more desperate. More deliberate.

People are reaching out because they want to do something real. Because watching from a distance is hard. Because prayer feels like the most honest response available.

It is.

---

Scripture says it clearly:

*"Pray for the peace of Jerusalem: may those who love you be secure."* — Psalm 122:6

That verse was not written in a peaceful season. It was written by someone who understood that prayer is not passive. It is presence.

Placing a prayer in Jerusalem is a small act. But it is a real one.

Your prayer crosses the distance between where you are and where your heart already is. It reaches the Western Wall. It rests at the Garden Tomb. It sits in the garden of Gethsemane, where Jesus himself once knelt and prayed through the impossible.

---

We place every prayer with care, as if it were our own.

No rush. No ceremony. Just a real moment at a real place, by a real person who takes your prayer seriously.

That is what we do. And it does not stop — not in quiet seasons, not in difficult ones.

If your heart is in Jerusalem right now, we will carry what is in it.

From your heart to God's ear.

**[Place a Prayer for Jerusalem Today]**

# Visual Direction: Garden Tomb Content

## Video Specifications

**Duration:** 45-60 seconds (allows time to feel the peace, not rushed)

**Mood:**
- Peaceful, not ominous
- Contemplative, not sad
- Sacred, not "spooky"
- Real, not overly cinematic
- The feeling of stepping into stillness

**Audio Direction:**
- Primary option: Minimal ambient sound (gentle bird song, subtle wind through trees, faint water if present)
- Alternative option: Silence with occasional bird song
- **Avoid:** Heavy instrumental music, dramatic effects, modern urban sounds, anything that breaks the peace

**Style:**
- Real photography, not stock footage
- Natural lighting (golden hour or early morning)
- Handheld or slow camera movement (not static, but meditative)
- Authentic sacred space, not theatrical

---

## What to Capture (Key Shots)

### Shot 1: Entry/Threshold (5 seconds)
- Approaching the tomb entrance
- Light filtering through olive trees
- Gateway between noise and silence
- Transition from outside world to sacred space

### Shot 2: Inside the Tomb Chamber (15 seconds)
- Moving into the cool stone chamber
- Soft morning light on ancient walls
- Stone texture and age evident
- Sense of entering something timeless
- Peaceful, not claustrophobic

### Shot 3: Garden Pathway (15 seconds)
- Walking through the garden area
- Olive trees overhead
- Light and shadow play
- The living, growing quality of the garden
- Sense of wandering, contemplating

### Shot 4: Detail Moments (10 seconds)
- Close-up of stone texture (hands touching wall, light on rock)
- Olive tree branches with light
- Water feature if present
- Bird at rest
- Something intimate that shows reverence

### Shot 5: Closing Moment (5 seconds)
- Return to stillness
- Settling into a peaceful place
- Sense of arrival, not departure
- Contemplative, inviting pause

## Blog Cover Image

**Recommended:** One of these approaches:

**Option A:** Early morning light entering the tomb (shot 2)
- Shows the sacred light quality
- Indicates: stillness, peace, antiquity
- Inviting without being too dramatic

**Option B:** Garden pathway with olive trees (shot 3)
- Shows: life, growth, contemplation

**Option C:** Stone and light detail (shot 4)

## Color Palette Reference

**Use Brand Colors:**
- **Jerusalem Gold** (#C4A265) - Warm, ancient, sacred
- **Parchment** (#F5F0E1) - Light, peaceful, accessible
- **Olive Green** (#6B7F3B) - Natural, grounding, garden

**Application:**
- Avoid color grading that adds warmth beyond natural golden hour
- Preserve natural stone tones
- Let light be the primary color element
- Subtle green from foliage should be present, not muted

---

## Cinematography Notes

**Camera Movement:**
- Slow, intentional pans and tilts
- Walking through space (not stationary shots)
- Meditative pace (5-10 seconds per shot minimum)
- No quick cuts or jittery movement

**Focus & Depth:**
- Use depth of field to isolate peaceful moments
- Keep sacred elements in focus
- Soft background blur to create intimacy
- Show scale (person in space) in some shots

**Lighting:**
- Prefer morning/golden hour light
- Show how light changes the space
- Shadows should be soft, not harsh
- Emphasize the illumination quality of the space

---

## Production Considerations

**If Using Existing Footage:**
- Select clips that are slow, contemplative, peaceful
- Edit to remove any people or crowded moments
- Color grade for warmth and peace
- Ensure audio is minimal/ambient only

**If Commissioning New Footage:**
- Recommend 8-10 AM shooting window (best light)
- Clear day (not overcast - need that golden light)
- Minimal/no human activity in background
- Bring audio equipment for quality ambient recording

---

## Editing Guidelines

- Fade to black at start and end (2-3 seconds each)
- Gentle transitions between shots (cross-fade, not cuts)
- Keep pacing slow - let each moment breathe
- End on a moment of stillness, not movement
- Final frame should invite pause, not action

---

## Success Looks Like

✅ Viewer feels peaceful watching, not rushed
✅ Sacred quality is evident without being theatrical
✅ The space feels real and inviting (not museum-like)
✅ Light is beautiful but natural
✅ Audio enhances peace (doesn't distract from it)
✅ Viewer wants to sit in stillness after watching
✅ The 45-60 second duration feels complete, not truncated

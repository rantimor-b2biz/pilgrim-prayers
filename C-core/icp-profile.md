# ICP Profile (Ideal Customer Profile)

> The ideal Pilgrim Prayers user — believers who long to place their prayers at holy sites but cannot be there in person.

---

## Overview

**Who they are:** Bible-believing Christians (ages 35–75), based in the US, Europe, South America, and Southeast Asia. They carry a deep emotional connection to Jerusalem and the Holy Land.

**What they want:** To feel spiritually present at sacred places — to know their prayer was placed with care, by a real person, at a real holy site.

**What's stopping them:** Health, finances, distance, or life circumstances prevent them from traveling. Online religious services often feel impersonal or untrustworthy.

## Psychographics

### What They Value
- Closeness to God and spiritual connection
- Authenticity — real actions, not empty gestures
- Trust and transparency in religious services
- Feeling seen, heard, and supported

### What They Fear
- That their prayers won't be taken seriously or handled with care
- That online religious services are impersonal or exploitative
- Feeling spiritually distant from holy places they hold dear
- Being forgotten or unheard

### What Motivates Them
- A desire for hope, healing, and reassurance
- Emotional and spiritual connection to Jerusalem
- The comfort of knowing their prayer is physically placed at a sacred site
- A sense of closure and spiritual presence

## Their Day-to-Day

### A Typical Day
They carry their faith into every part of life. Prayer is personal and important to them. They think about Jerusalem and the Holy Land with reverence and longing. They may be dealing with health challenges, loss, uncertainty, or gratitude — and they want their prayers to reach a place that feels sacred.

### Frustrations They Experience
1. Feeling spiritually distant from holy places they deeply connect with
2. Online religious services that feel generic, transactional, or untrustworthy
3. The gap between wanting to be in Jerusalem and the reality that they cannot travel

### Wins They Celebrate
1. Receiving confirmation that their prayer was placed at a holy site
2. Feeling a sense of peace, hope, or spiritual connection
3. Sharing a meaningful faith experience with loved ones

## How They Make Decisions

### Information Sources
Where do they learn about new solutions?
- Church communities and faith groups
- Word of mouth from fellow believers
- Christian media, social media, and faith-based content
- Recommendations from pastors or spiritual leaders

### Decision Factors
What matters when they evaluate options?
1. **Trust** — Is this real? Are there real people at real locations?
2. **Proof** — Will I receive confirmation that my prayer was placed?
3. **Reverence** — Is my prayer treated as sacred, not as a transaction?

### Objections They Have
What stops them from using the service?
1. "Is this real or just another online scam?" — We show real photos, real locations, and provide proof of placement
2. "Will my prayer actually be placed?" — Transparent process with confirmation and documentation
3. "Is this replacing my own prayer life?" — We complement personal prayer; we never replace it

## Sample Persona

### Margaret — 62, Texas

**Background:**
Margaret is a devoted Bible-believing Christian. She's always dreamed of visiting Jerusalem but her husband's health makes travel impossible. Her faith is the center of her life, and she prays daily for her family's wellbeing. She heard about Pilgrim Prayers through her church group.

**Goals:**
- To feel spiritually present at the Western Wall and other holy sites
- To place a prayer for her husband's health at a sacred place

**Challenges:**
- Cannot travel due to her husband's health condition
- Skeptical of online services — needs proof and transparency
- Wants it to feel personal, not like a mass-produced service

**Quote that captures them:**
> "I can't be there myself, but knowing someone placed my prayer with care... it brings me peace."

# Newsletter Skill

Turn a blog post into a letter from Jerusalem.

## When to Use

- Converting an approved weekly blog post into newsletter format
- Writing email subject lines for Pilgrim Prayers
- Formatting email content for subscriber engagement

---

## Newsletter Basics

**What works in email for Pilgrim Prayers:**
- Personal, intimate tone — like a letter from a friend
- One story, one feeling, one action
- Short enough to read in 2-3 minutes
- Opens with the strongest emotional moment from the blog post

**Email length sweet spot:** 300-500 words
- Under 300: Feels incomplete
- Over 500: People stop reading
- Perfect: Enough to feel, short enough to finish

---

## Subject Line Formulas

The subject line determines if the email is opened. Max 50 characters.

### Formula 1: Place + Time
```
"A morning prayer at the Western Wall"
"Sunset over the Mount of Olives"
"Dawn in the Garden of Gethsemane"
```

### Formula 2: Story Hint
```
"She wrote three words on her prayer"
"A folded note, placed between ancient stones"
"He couldn't travel. His prayer could."
```

### Formula 3: Scripture Echo
```
"Where does my help come from?"
"Be still and know..."
"I will lift up my eyes"
```

### Formula 4: Direct + Warm
```
"Your prayer has a place in Jerusalem"
"A moment of quiet from the Holy Land"
"This week from Jerusalem"
```

### Formula 5: Seasonal
```
"Easter morning at the empty tomb"
"Christmas Eve in Bethlehem"
"Spring has come to Jerusalem"
```

### What Never Works
```
❌ "Pilgrim Prayers Newsletter #14"
❌ "INCREDIBLE news from Jerusalem!!!"
❌ "Don't miss this week's story"
❌ "Last chance to place your prayer"
❌ ALL CAPS or excessive punctuation!!!
```

---

## Preview Text (Preheader)

40-90 characters. Complements the subject — never repeats it.

| Subject | Preview |
|---------|---------|
| "A morning prayer at the Western Wall" | "The stones were still cool from the night." |
| "She wrote three words on her prayer" | "A prayer from Ohio, placed in Jerusalem with care." |
| "Easter morning at the empty tomb" | "The stone was rolled away. And hope returned." |

## Email Structure Template

```markdown
[Greeting]

[Hook — the strongest moment from the blog post, 1-2 sentences]

[Story core — condensed to 2-3 paragraphs max]

[Emotional bridge — connect the story to the reader personally]

[CTA — one gentle invitation]

[Sign-off]

---
[Footer]
```

---

## Greeting Options

Keep it warm and simple:

```
"Dear friend,"
"Friend,"
"Dear [First Name],"
```

Never:
```
❌ "Dear valued subscriber,"
❌ "Hello Pilgrim Prayers community!"
❌ "Greetings in Christ,"  (too formal)
```

---

## Sign-off Options

```
The Pilgrim Prayers Team"

"From Jerusalem with prayer,
Pilgrim Prayers"

"Walking with you in faith,
The Pilgrim Prayers Team"

"Until next week,
Pilgrim Prayers"
```

## CTA Guidelines

Only ONE call to action per email.

| CTA Type | Button/Link Text | When to Use |
|----------|-----------------|-------------|
| Read full story | "Read the full story →" | Email is an excerpt; full post has more |
| Submit a prayer | "Place your prayer in Jerusalem →" | Story is emotionally complete; reader is moved |
| Share | "Share this with someone who needs it →" | Story is about community, comfort, or gifting |

**CTA Tone:**
```
✅ "Whenever you're ready, we're here."
✅ "If there's a prayer on your heart, it has a place in Jerusalem."
✅ "Read the rest of this story on our blog →"
❌ "ACT NOW!"
❌ "Limited spots available!"
❌ "Don't miss your chance!"
```

## Adaptation Rules

When converting a blog post to email:

### What to Keep
- The strongest opening moment
- The emotional core of the story
- Scripture references (if used naturally)
- The sense of place and presence

### What to Cut
- Background context that's not essential
- Secondary stories or tangents
- Detailed historical information (save for the blog)
- Long descriptive passages — condense to essentials

### What to Add
- Personal greeting
- Direct address ("you", "your prayer", "your heart")
- A warmer, more intimate tone
- A clear CTA that's not in the blog post
- Sign-off and footer

### What to Change
- Third person → Second person ("A prayer was placed" → "Your prayer could be there")
- Observational → Personal ("The Wall stands..." → "Imagine standing before this Wall...")
- Story mode → Letter mode

## Quality Checklist

Before delivering:

- [ ] Subject line under 50 characters?
- [ ] Preview text complements (not repeats) the subject?
- [ ] Email body under 500 words?
- [ ] Warm greeting included?
- [ ] One clear story — emotionally complete?
- [ ] Only ONE CTA?
- [ ] CTA is warm, not pushy?
- [ ] Personal sign-off?
- [ ] Footer with unsubscribe link?
- [ ] Matches voice DNA?
- [ ] Reads like a letter, not a marketing email?

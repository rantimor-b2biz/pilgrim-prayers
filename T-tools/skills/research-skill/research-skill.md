# Research Skill

How the Research Agent packages intelligence for the content pipeline.

---

## Output 1: Weekly Research Brief

**Purpose:** Feed the Storyteller with everything needed to write a timely, grounded, emotionally resonant post.
**When:** Every Sunday, before the Storyteller writes.
**Saved to:** `O-output/[project-folder]/research-brief.md`
**Read by:** Storyteller Agent, Gatekeeper Agent

### Template

```markdown
# Research Brief: [Week Topic Title]

**Week:** [Number from content calendar]
**Topic:** [Topic from content calendar]
**Post Structure:** [FIELD / PLACE / BELIEVER / SEASON / QUIET / HISTORY]
**Holy Land Site:** [Primary location]
**Date:** [Research date]

---

## Site Intelligence

**Season right now:** [What the weather and light are like at this site this week]
**Activity level:** [Busy with pilgrims / Quiet / Closed / Special event]
**Recent news:** [Anything notable happening at or near this site]
**Sensory snapshot:** [2-3 sentences: what you'd see, hear, smell, feel if you were standing there today]

---

## Scripture Connections

| Passage | Connection | Emotional Angle |
|---------|-----------|-----------------|
| [Book Chapter:Verse] | [How it connects to the site] | [What feeling it evokes] |
| [Book Chapter:Verse] | [How it connects to the site] | [What feeling it evokes] |
| [Book Chapter:Verse] | [How it connects to the site] | [What feeling it evokes] |

**Recommended lead scripture:** [Which passage best fits this week's post structure and emotional target]

---

## Story Hooks

**Human angle:** [1-2 sentences describing a real testimony type or prayer need category that connects to this site/topic. Not a specific person's story, but a pattern: "Many believers write about..." or "A common prayer at this site is..."]

**Historical narrative moment:** [A specific historical fact about this site told as a story beat, not a textbook entry. Something the Storyteller can weave into the post.]

**Surprise element:** [A "did you know" fact that would make Margaret pause and lean in. Something unexpected, beautiful, or moving about this site.]

---

## Trending Context

**What Evangelicals are talking about this week:** [1-2 sentences about what's trending in the Evangelical space that connects to this topic. A popular book, a sermon clip, a social media moment, a news story.]

**Cultural moment:** [Any broader cultural event (holiday, season, news cycle) that makes this topic more timely or emotionally relevant right now.]

**Similar content performing well:** [What posts, videos, or podcasts on similar themes are resonating in the Evangelical space this week. What angles are they taking?]

---

## Visual Suggestions

**What to photograph right now:** [What a photographer standing at this site this week would capture. Season-specific: spring wildflowers, summer heat haze, autumn golden light, winter rain on stone.]

**Key visual elements:** [2-3 specific visual details: a type of stone, a plant, a light quality, a texture, a color that defines this site in this season.]

---

## Storyteller Notes

**Recommended emotional angle:** [One sentence: what single feeling should the reader walk away with?]

**Lean into:** [What aspects of this topic will resonate most with the audience right now]

**Avoid:** [What to steer clear of this week, based on current events, audience mood, or brand boundaries]

**Audience insight:** [What the Research Agent knows about what believers are feeling this week that should shape the post's tone. Are they grieving? Hopeful? Anxious? Grateful?]
```

### Writing Rules for the Brief

1. **Story-first, not data-first.** The Storyteller needs story hooks, not spreadsheets. Frame every insight as a narrative seed.

2. **Specific, not generic.** "It's cold in Jerusalem" is useless. "The stones of the Western Wall are wet from overnight rain, and the plaza is nearly empty at dawn" is a story.

3. **One recommended angle.** Don't give the Storyteller 10 options. Give them THE angle, with reasoning.

4. **Scripture as story, not sermon.** Connect passages to the site emotionally, not theologically. "This is where it happened" beats "The theological significance of..."

5. **Margaret test.** Would Margaret (62, Texas, Evangelical, husband's health prevents travel) find this interesting? Would it make her feel closer to the Holy Land? If not, cut it.

## Output 3: Content Opportunity Alert

**Purpose:** Flag a timely content opportunity that shouldn't wait for the monthly report.
**When:** As they arise.
**Saved to:** `B-brain/content-opportunities/[YYYY-MM-DD]-[slug].md`
**Read by:** Storyteller Agent, Gatekeeper Agent

### Template

```markdown
# Content Opportunity Alert

**Date:** [Date]
**Flagged by:** Research Agent

---

## The Opportunity

**What:** [One sentence: what's happening]

**Why now:** [The trending topic + the Holy Land connection + the timely moment]

**The audience angle:** [Why Margaret and our readers would care about this right now]

---

## Recommended Action

- [ ] Swap this week's planned topic for this one
- [ ] Add as a bonus mid-week post
- [ ] Save for next week (swap with planned topic)
- [ ] Weave into the already-planned topic as a secondary thread
- [ ] Note for future planning (not urgent)

**Urgency:** [This week / Next 2 weeks / Can plan ahead]

---

## Quick Research

**Key facts:** [3-5 bullet points]
**Scripture connection:** [1-2 passages]
**Story hook:** [One sentence]
**Source:** [Where you found this]
```

## Output 4: M-memory Updates

**Purpose:** Feed audience insights and content performance data back into the system.
**When:** After each publish cycle.
**Updated files:**
- `M-memory/feedback.md` — What the audience responded to
- `M-memory/learning-log.md` — What patterns are emerging

### What to Log

**In feedback.md:**
- Post title and date
- Engagement metrics (relative: high/medium/low)
- Notable reader comments or reactions
- Newsletter open rate and click rate (relative)
- Prayer submissions tied to the post (if trackable)

**In learning-log.md:**
- Patterns: "Posts about [topic] consistently perform [well/poorly]"
- Insights: "The audience responds most to [specific element]"
- Corrections: "We assumed [X] but the data shows [Y]"

## Research Rhythm

| Day | Task |
|-----|------|
| **Sunday** | Read content calendar for this week. Research the site, trends, audience pulse. Save research-brief.md. |
| **Monday-Friday** | Monitor for content opportunities. Flag if found. |
| **After Publish** | Track performance. Update M-memory. |
| **First of Month** | Create monthly trend report. Review calendar. Recommend adjustments. |

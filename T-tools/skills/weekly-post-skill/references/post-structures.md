# 6 Rotating Post Structures

Use these on a 6-week rotation to keep content varied and fresh.

## Structure 1: Story from the Field
**Use when:** A prayer was placed, a moment happened, something real occurred

```
[Opening: A specific moment — placing a prayer, a sunrise, a quiet scene]

[The story: What happened, who was involved, what was felt]

[The meaning: Why this moment matters — connect to faith]

[Scripture thread: A verse that echoes the moment]

[Gentle close: Invitation to be part of the next story]
```

**Example theme:** "This morning we placed a prayer at the Garden Tomb..."

---

## Structure 2: Holy Place Deep Dive
**Use when:** Featuring a specific biblical site

```
[Opening: Vivid scene-setting at the location]

[Biblical story: What happened here — told freshly, not like a textbook]

[What it's like today: Real sensory details — stones, light, sound, air]

[The connection: Why this place matters to someone praying from afar]

[Gentle close: Your prayer can reach this place too]
```

**Example theme:** "Gethsemane — The Garden Where Jesus Prayed His Hardest Prayer"

---

## Structure 3: Believer's Story
**Use when:** Sharing a testimony or composite story of someone who sent a prayer

```
[Opening: Introduce the person — name, place, situation]

[Their need: What brought them to Pilgrim Prayers — health, loss, hope, gratitude]

[The prayer: What they asked for (respectfully, without exposing private details)]

[The placement: How and where their prayer was placed]

[The feeling: What they said afterward — peace, connection, closure]

[Gentle close: Your story matters too]
```

**Example theme:** "Margaret from Texas couldn't travel to Jerusalem. But her prayer did."

---

## Structure 4: Season or Holy Day Reflection
**Use when:** Tied to Evangelical calendar moments, seasons, or significant dates

```
[Opening: What this season/day means — told through a scene in Jerusalem]

[Biblical roots: The story behind the observance]

[Jerusalem today: How this moment feels in the Holy Land right now]

[Personal reflection: What this means for believers everywhere]

[Gentle close: Mark this season with a prayer placed in the Holy Land]
```

**Example theme:** "Easter morning at the Garden Tomb — where the stone was rolled away"

---

## Structure 5: The Quiet Moment
**Use when:** A contemplative, meditative post — slower pace, deeper feeling

```
[Opening: A single, still image — a note in a wall, light on stone, a hand in prayer]

[Reflection: What this quiet moment holds — no rush, no agenda]

[Scripture: A Psalm or Gospel verse that fits the silence]

[The invitation: Sometimes prayer doesn't need words — just a place]

[Gentle close: We'll carry your prayer to that quiet place]
```

**Example theme:** "What happens in the silence between prayers"

---

## Structure 6: History & Faith
**Use when:** Connecting ancient history to living faith

```
[Opening: A striking historical detail — a date, an event, a discovery]

[The story: What happened here — told as narrative, not lecture]

[The through-line: How this connects across 2,000 years to today]

[The living faith: This isn't just history — people still pray here]

[Gentle close: You are part of this 2,000-year story of faith]
```

**Example theme:** "2,000 years of prayers at this wall — and yours can be next"

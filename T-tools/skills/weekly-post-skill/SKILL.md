# Weekly Post Skill

Tell the story of Jerusalem — one week at a time.

## Instructions

### Step 1: Understand the Topic
Read from content calendar (B-brain folder) for this week's theme.
Check Voice DNA and ICP (C-core folder) before writing.

### Step 2: Choose Post Structure
Use one of 6 rotating structures (see [references/post-structures.md](references/post-structures.md)):
1. Story from the Field
2. Holy Place Deep Dive
3. Believer's Story
4. Season or Holy Day Reflection
5. The Quiet Moment
6. History & Faith

### Step 3: Write the Post
- **Opening**: Place the reader somewhere specific (2-3 sentences)
- **Body**: One story, one place, one feeling (500-800 words total)
- **Scripture**: Woven naturally, not as sermon
- **Close**: Gentle invitation, warm tone

See [references/writing-rules.md](references/writing-rules.md) for detailed guidelines.

### Step 4: Quality Check
Before delivering, verify:
- [ ] Opens with specific scene-setting
- [ ] One clear story (not collection of ideas)
- [ ] Scripture woven naturally
- [ ] Warm, faithful tone (not preachy)
- [ ] Gentle invitation (under 20% of post)
- [ ] 500-800 words
- [ ] Imagery suggestions included
- [ ] Aligned with content calendar theme

### Step 5: Save Output
Save draft in O-output folder for project.
Log what worked in M-memory learning log.

## Common Issues

**Issue:** Post feels too preachy
**Solution:** Use "show don't tell" - describe scenes, moments, sensory details instead of theological statements

**Issue:** Invitation feels too salesy
**Solution:** Keep final 20% gentle. Use phrases like "If there's a prayer on your heart today, it has a place in Jerusalem" instead of "Submit your prayer now!"

**Issue:** Multiple topics in one post
**Solution:** Focus on one place, one story, one feeling per post

## Examples

**Example 1: Story from the Field**
User says: "Write about placing prayers at the Western Wall this morning"

Actions:
1. Use Structure 1 (Story from the Field)
2. Open with specific morning scene at the Wall
3. Tell what happened with sensory details
4. Weave in relevant Psalm or Gospel verse
5. Close with gentle invitation

Result: 600-word post with warmth and specific details

# Feedback Log

Track feedback on your content — what resonated, what didn't, and what your audience actually said.

---

## Why Track Feedback

> "Your audience teaches you how to serve them better."

Feedback is external signal. Unlike the learning-log (internal patterns), this captures:
- Comments on your posts
- DMs and replies
- Engagement patterns
- Direct audience input

---

## How to Use This File

**After publishing:** Log notable feedback
**Weekly:** Review for patterns
**Monthly:** Extract insights to learning-log

---

## Patterns to Watch For

**High engagement signals:**
- Topics that get saved/bookmarked
- Posts that generate DMs
- Content that gets shared by others

**Low engagement signals:**
- Posts with no comments
- Content that feels "fine" but forgettable

**Audience language:**
- Phrases they use to describe their problems
- Words that appear in positive comments
- Questions they keep asking

## Critical Writing Standards (Project Feedback)

### NO EM DASHES
**Feedback:** Remove all em dashes (—) from all content
**Implementation:** Use alternatives instead:
- Parentheses: () for explanatory clauses
- Colons: : for introductions
- Hyphens: - for separators and dates
- Period splits: Breaking sentences for emphasis

**Status:** Applied to all publication materials 2026-02-24
**Remember:** Check and remove all em dashes in EVERY piece of content going forward

## CRITICAL AUDIENCE INSIGHT: Language Preference

**Feedback:** Never use the word "evangelical" in copy — many audience members reject this label even though they ARE evangelicals.

**Preferred Language:** "Bible-believing Christians"

**Applied To:**
- ✅ All blog posts and newsletters
- ✅ All paid search ads
- ✅ Email subject lines
- ✅ Website copy
- ✅ Social media

**Status:** Implemented 2026-02-26

**Reason:** Audience research shows this term creates instant resistance and closes doors. "Bible-believing Christians" feels personal and welcoming instead of labeled/categorized.
